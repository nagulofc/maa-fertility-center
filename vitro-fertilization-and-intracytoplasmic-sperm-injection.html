What is procedure for the surgery

Intracytoplasmic Sperm Injection (ICSI) is a specialised form of In Vitro Fertilisation (IVF) that is used primarily for the treatment of critical cases of male-factor infertility. This process involves the injection of a single sperm directly into a mature egg. Doctors at Maa fertility will monitor the development of eggs. ICSI is performed when the egg is ready to be retrieved.

  • Sperm retrieval through one of the many ways.
  • Injecting Sperm into the Egg.
  • Monitoring the Embryo for signs of growth and development.
  • Transfer of Embryos to a women’s womb.

After surgery Benefits

ICSI is definitely worth considering in case of ejaculation problems and other issues with male infertility. Couples experiencing these problems can definitely get the benefits of ICSI treatment.

  • ICSI can be performed even if the man has undergone a vasectomy.
  • Viable sperm can be extracted directly from testicles using special sperm retrieval methods and injected into the egg.
  • This method has low associated risks.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is the life term of the washed sperm?

    Washed sperm, in general, live for 6-12 hours, but, sometimes as long as 24 to 48 hours. It’s not advised to be kept for too long. Thus, the timing for IUI is so important that it is advisable to do insemination within 3 hours.

  • Can the sperm fall out?

    The sperm doesn’t fall out once it is injected into the uterus.

  • Can IUI be done at home?

    The sperm needs to be washed and thus IUI shouldn’t be done at home. Patients should not attempt to do IUI at home as getting semen or air into the uterus can cause physio-pathological complications.
