  • Home
  • Frozen Embryo Transfer (FET)






What is procedure for the surgery

Frozen Embryo Transfer, referred to as FET, is the method of using a frozen embryo from a previous IVF cycle. It’s gaining popularity due to its success rates and safety. A woman undergoes the standard IVF procedure during this procedure, but instead of transferring the embryos back into the uterus in the same cycle, the embryos are frozen for use in a future cycle.

  • A woman can freeze her own eggs or choose to use Donor Eggs.
  • Eggs are transferred in a later cycle.
  • Reduces the effect of Desynchronization.

After surgery Benefits

FET has advanced in recent years, it resembles natural conception and results in increased pregnancy, decreased miscarriages, and healthier babies.

Less Medication Since the stimulation phase is in a prior cycle, there is no egg retrieval requiring anaesthesia

Less Stress During the fresh cycle, factors like stimulation response, egg development, and embryo growth will be considered. FET cycles are less stressful, patients may select the day of their transfer months prior, which will then be used to determine their cycle initiation date.

  • Lesser use of medicines.
  • Lesser Stress
  • Simpler process.
